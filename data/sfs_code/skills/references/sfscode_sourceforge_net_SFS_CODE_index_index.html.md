Simulating Finite Sites under COmplex Demographic Events S imulating F inite S ites under CO mplex D emographic E vents SFS_CODE can perform forward population genetic simulations under a general Wright-Fisher model with arbitrary migration, demographic, selective, and mutational effects. This program was developed by Ryan Hernandez (ryan.hernandez [at] ucsf [dot] edu). The project began while he was a graduate student in the Bustamante lab at Cornell University , and maintenance continued through his posdoc with Molly Przeworski at the University of Chicago . Ryan is currently an assistant professor at UCSF . Acknowledgments Many thanks to Lawrence Uricchio (a graduate student in the lab) who created sfs_coder , a nice python-based front-end for SFS_CODE! This allows the user to perform simulations based on any desired region of the human genome, including the inferred local recombination map, as well as a distribution of deleterious selection coefficients on coding and conserved-noncoding loci. Many thanks to my β -users, especially Ryan Gutenkunst , who helped me to debug and stress-test this program, and to the National Science Foundation for support through a Minority Postdoctoral Research Fellowship while at the University of Chicago.
