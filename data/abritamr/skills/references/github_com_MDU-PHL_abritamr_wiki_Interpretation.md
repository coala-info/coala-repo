## Gene detection

![](https://camo.githubusercontent.com/9000f7e33763d85bd43af7082d2ca09404b7c613bc4c2548e253a556da656907/68747470733a2f2f62696f696e666f726d61746963732e6d64752e756e696d656c622e6564752e61752f7e6b68686f722f696d616765732f7265636f766572792e706e67)
*Figure 1: Possible gene recovery*

Detection of genes by abriTAMR can fall into two categories. Full gene recovery is considered when there is an exact match, that is 100% identitiy across 100% of a protein in the AMRfinder database or when a sequence on a single contig spans > 90% of protein in the AMRfinder database with >90% identity but less than 100%. These are identified by a \*. Partial gene recovery occurs when between 50% and 90% of a protein in the AMRfinder database is covered by a contig at >90% identity. Based on validation outined in V0113, aac(6')-Ib-cr will only reported as detected if a full match (100% to the allele in the AMRfinder DB) occurs, otherwise it will be reported as a partial gene and may require confirmation.

### Acceptance/rejection criteria

*Accepted*

Full gene recovery has occurred meaning that >90% of a gene was recovered with >90% protein identity to a gene in the database. It should be noted that this does not preclude the presence of multiple copies of the same gene.

*Confirmation required*

Partial gene recovery has occurred. Partial matches indicate that between 50% and 90% of the length of gene has been recovered in a single contig with > 90% protein identity to a gene in the database. Genes that fall into this category will be referred to bioinformatics for further examination and confirmation of the presence of the gene.

*Rejected*

Genes that are recovered with less than 90% protein identity to a gene in the database and/or with < 50% of gene present will not be reported.
