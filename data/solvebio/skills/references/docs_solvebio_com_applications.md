# Applications Overview[¶](#applications-overview "Permanent link")

Please upgrade your client libraries!

Application support was introduced in **solvebio-python v2.3.0** and **solvebio-r v2.2.0**. Please update your client libraries accordingly.

SolveBio provides an API-driven "unified data layer" accessible by developers and scientists across your organization. The SolveBio platform can be further leveraged by building interactive apps on top of its data APIs. This enables a "micro-app" architecture that scales R&D in a few ways:

* Build **reproducible apps** that answer scientific questions that can be answered with a shareable URL rather than a one-off script.
* Create an **ecosystem of reusable components** that make app development much easier within and across scientific domains.
* Take advantage of **shared components and apps** developed by other SolveBio users.

Scientific applications are typically bundled with static snapshots of data, making them bloated and cumbersome. On SolveBio, apps can be incredibly lightweight and easy to deploy and maintain. An app can be represented by a single Python or R script, and deployed to any app hosting system on-premises or in the cloud.

## Step 1: Create Your App[¶](#step-1-create-your-app "Permanent link")

Apps and data are automatically secured by SolveBio's built-in user and group access controls. App authentication is based on the [OAuth2 Authorization Code flow](https://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc6749#section-1.3.1) and assumes a *public* client type.

Every app has a unique client ID to identify itself in the authorization flow. The client ID can be made public.

[Learn how to create and manage apps →](managing/)

## Step 2: Develop Your App[¶](#step-2-develop-your-app "Permanent link")

SolveBio recommends the following frameworks to build apps:

* [R Shiny](https://shiny.rstudio.com/)
* [Python Dash](https://plot.ly/products/dash/)

While you can technically use any app framework to develop an app, you may have to integrate OAuth2 support yourself if you choose to do so ([contact SolveBio Support](../support/) for help).

[Learn how to develop apps →](developing/)

## Step 3: Deploy Your App[¶](#step-3-deploy-your-app "Permanent link")

Apps can be deployed on-premises or in the cloud. Users of R/Shiny can deploy apps to their local instance of [Shiny Server Pro](https://www.rstudio.com/products/shiny-server-pro/) if one is available. Alternatively, the cloud-based [ShinyApps.io](https://www.shinyapps.io/) can be used to host R/Shiny apps for SolveBio. Apps developed using Python/Dash can be [deployed to Heroku, AWS, on-premises (using Plotly On-Premise)](https://plot.ly/dash/deployment) or on SolveBio's App Server (coming soon).

## Step 4: Share Your App[¶](#step-4-share-your-app "Permanent link")

Sharing your new app should be simple once deployed. All deployed apps should have a URL that can be shared with colleagues.

SolveBio has developed an app launcher which is available to all SolveBio end-users. This enables you to see all your available apps and launch them right from SolveBio. [Contact SolveBio Support](../support/) to add your app to the launcher.
