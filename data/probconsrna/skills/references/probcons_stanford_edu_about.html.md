PROBCONS: Probabilistic Consistency-based Multiple Alignment of Amino Acid Sequences Probabilistic Consistency-based Multiple Alignment of Amino Acid Sequences &nbsp; RUN &nbsp; ABOUT &nbsp; DOWNLOAD &nbsp; HELP &nbsp; About the P ROB C ONS project P ROB C ONS is a novel tool for generating multiple alignments of protein sequences. Using a combination of probabilistic modeling and consistency-based alignment techniques, P ROB C ONS has achieved the highest accuracies of all alignment methods to date. On the BAliBASE benchmark alignment database, alignments produced by P ROB C ONS show statistically significant improvement over current programs, containing an average of 7% more correctly aligned columns than those of T-Coffee, 11% more correctly aligned columns than those of CLUSTAL W, and 14% more correctly aligned columns than those of DIALIGN. P ROB C ONS was developed by Chuong Do in collaboration with Michael Brudno in the research group of Serafim Batzoglou, Department of Computer Science, Stanford University. Further information The methods used in P ROB C ONS are described in the following paper: Do, C.B., Mahabhashyam, M.S.P., Brudno, M., and Batzoglou, S. 2005. PROBCONS: Probabilistic Consistency-based Multiple Sequence Alignment. Genome Research 15: 330-340.
