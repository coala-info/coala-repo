## Recent Work

The lipidome in alcohol-related liver disease (ALD).

In this [publication](https://doi.org/10.1053/j.gastro.2023.02.023), we show that sphingomyelins (SMs) are depleted with liver fibrosis, both, in the liver and in circulation. The depletion is stronger in ‘pure’ ALD than in NAFLD-like ALD, and it is associated with an elevated risk of disease progression. Moreover, we show by Mendelian randomization that ALD has a causal link to lower blood SM level in general population.

The pathogenesis of type 1 diabetes.

I have [reviewed](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11892-020-01326-8), how the lipidome is affected at the onset of type 1 diabetes. This publication is based on an open-access [database of findings](https://github.com/tommi-s/T1D-lipidome) from relevant scientific articles on the topic, which I created and made available for further research and updating. Read the [publication](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11892-020-01326-8) for a shortlist of relevant lipids that I propose as a starting point for further validation studies.

lipidomeR is a new tool for lipidome-wide visualization of statistical associations.

The tool creates integrative heatmaps of patterns in the entire measured lipidome. The package can be installed in R with the command 'install.packages("lipidomeR")'. See [lipidomeR.org](https://lipidomeR.org) for reproducible examples, the [manuscript preprint](https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.03.16.994061) for guidance on the systematic interpretion of the results, and [CRAN](https://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/lipidomeR) for documentation of the tool.

## Introduction

I am a data scientist with a background in machine learning and computational sciences, and a specific focus in molecular medicine.

While based in Stockholm, I work as researcher at the academic level of Assistant Professor at [Steno Diabetes Center Copenhagen (SDCC)](https://www.sdcc.dk/english/). SDCC is the largest center of diabetes treatment in Scandinavia and it is [funded](https://steno.dk/%20) by the [Novo Nordisk Foundation](https://novonordiskfonden.dk/en/projects-and-initiatives/steno-diabetes-centre/).

I am affiliated with the [Translational Type 1 Diabetes Research](https://www.sdcc.dk/english/research/clinical-research/research-groups/translational-type-1-diabetes-research/Pages/Presentation-of-Tranlational-type-1-diabetes-research.aspx) group, where I work as a bioinformatics and biostatistics specialist to process, analyze, visualize and understand complex omics data from the small molecule profiling platforms managed by the group. Moreover, I work 30 % as data science consultant to the [Clinical and Translational Research Department](https://www.sdcc.dk/english/research/clinical-research/Pages/default.aspx) in a single-handed advisory responsibility to 150+ researchers in five research groups.

During my career, I have acquired an in-depth knowledge of computational methods for data-driven analysis as well as a broad understanding of application areas in health and clinical research. Lately, the focus of my work has been in the development and application of computational methods for studying biomarkers of diabetes, its development, treatment and complications.

At SDCC, I have set up extensive computational pipelines for a streamlined processing, analysis and visualization of data from the team’s omics profiling platforms. While I continue to develop these solutions, I collaborate with clinicians, chemists, biologists and statisticians to help them understand complex research data and to uncover novel insights from measured molecular profiles in large clinical studies.

![Lipidomic analysis reveals sphingomyelin and phosphatidylcholine species associated with renal impairment and all-cause mortality in type 1 diabetes](img/portfolio/XS-Combined-Heatmaps-New-Specific-1.png)

In this large observational study of persons with type 1 diabetes, we found out that the blood levels of sphingomyelins (SMs) and alkyl-acyl phosphatidylcholines (PC-Os) and low-unsaturated middle-sized triacylglycerols (TGs) are reduced in macro-albuminuria. In further investigation with survival models, we found out that a lower level of SM(d41:1) was associated with higher mortality in the 5-year follow-up. This association remained, when adjusting to other factors, both, in the entire cohort as well as in the risk-factor-matched subset of the cohort.

Algorithmic tools used in this work include ordinary linear regression analysis, survival analysis with Cox proportional hazards regression, logistic regression with forward-selection within a nested cross-validation framework, and propensity score matching-style subsampling.

This work was a part of Dr. Nete Tofte's PhD and was presented at EASD 2018 and Kidney Week 2018.

**Lipidomic analysis reveals sphingomyelin and phosphatidylcholine species associated with renal impairment and all-cause mortality in type 1 diabetes**

Nete Tofte\*, Tommi Suvitaival\*, Linda Ahonen, Signe A. Winther, Simone Theilade, Marie Frimodt-Møller, Tarunveer S. Ahluwalia, Peter Rossing

*Scientific Reports* **9**, 16398 (2019)

<https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-52916-w>

![Lipidome as a predictive tool in progression to type 2 diabetes in Finnish men](img/portfolio/Suvitaival-2018-Figure_4-optimized.jpg)

In this large cohort study, we investigated, whether individual lipid species can predict the upcoming onset of type 2 diabetes in a high-risk population. We found out that lyso-phosphatidylcholine (LPC) 18:2 and certain phosphatidylcholine (PC) and triacylglycerol (TG) species improved the prediction, when compared to known risk factors. This was also observed in a subpopulation with no difference in fasting plasma glucose levels between disease progressors and non-progressors.

Algorithmic tools used in this study included logistic regression with forward selection within a rigorous nested cross-validation framework and a propensity score-style matching of study groups. The work was funded by the EU-FP7 project DEXLIFE. The study samples were kindly provided by Prof. [Markku Laakso](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=ZGCyRqoAAAAJ)

![Metabolomic assessment reveals alteration in polyols and branched chain amino acids associated with present and future renal impairment in a discovery cohort of 637 persons with type 1 diabetes](img/portfolio/0033_PROFIL--Metabolomics--PCor-EBICLasso-Network-Connected-Publication-Legends-B-1--190927-optimized.jpg)

In this large observational study of persons with type 1 diabetes, we found out that renal function dominates the landscape of the blood metabolome.

In a data driven association analysis with the graphical LASSO algorithm, we discovered that among the clinical factors, metabolite levels were most associated with glomerular filtration rate (eGFR). These findings were further investigated with survival analysis of outcomes of renal disease in the 5-year follow-up.

Algorithmic tools used in this work include the sparse inverse covariance matrix extimation with the graphical LASSO, network visualization, ordinary linear regression analysis, survival analysis with Cox proportional hazards regression, and propensity score matching-style subsampling.

This work was a part of Dr. Nete Tofte's PhD and was presented at EASD 2019.

![Multivariate multi-way analysis of multi-source data](img/portfolio/Huopaniemi-2010-Figure_2.jpeg)

In this methods developement paper, we presented a Bayesian approach for integrating data from multiple high-dimensional (i.e., "multivariate") measurement domains (i.e., "views") at the presence of experimental covariates (i.e., "ways"). For the data views (\(\mathbf{x}\) and \(\mathbf{y}\)), we constructed a latent variable representation that is view-specific (\(\mathbf{x}^{\text{lat}}\) and \(\mathbf{y}^{\text{lat}}\)) while the relevant signal shared by the views is integrated by a higher-level latent representation (\(\mathbf{z}\)), which responds to the covariates (\(\mathbf{a}\) and \(\mathbf{b}\)). This integrative machine learning model could be described as a hierarchical fusion of probabilistic factor analysis (FA), canonical correlation analysis (CCA) and analysis of variance (ANOVA).

This work was part of my doctoral studies and the work was presented at ISMB 2010.

**Multivariate multi-way analysis of multi-source data**

Ilkka Huopaniemi, Tommi Suvitaival, Janne Nikkilä, Matej Orešič, Samuel Kaski

*Bioinformatics*, **26** (12), i391–i398

<https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btq174>

![Cross-organism toxicogenomics with group factor analysis](img/portfolio/Suvitaival-2014-Figure_1.jpeg)

In this paper, we used a novel multi-view data integration method -- the group factor analysis (GFA) -- to modeling responses to treatments with an interest in identifying responses that are shared between cell lines and model organisms' organs. To infer interpretable associations, we extended the GFA model with a sparcity-inducing prior on the factor projections.

This work was part of my doctoral studies and we participated with the work in the CAMDA Challenge of ISMB 2013. The popular [MOFA package](https://www.embopress.org/doi/full/10.15252/msb.20178124) for multi-omics data integration is based on the GFA model.

**Cross-organism toxicogenomics with group factor analysis**

Tommi Suvitaival, Juuso A. Parkkinen, Seppo Virtanen, Samuel Kaski

Systems Biomedicine, **2** (4), 71-80

<https://doi.org/10.4161/sysb.29291>
