# The MOSCA project

[Jump to bottom](#wiki-pages-box)

João Sequeira edited this page May 17, 2023
·
[6 revisions](/iquasere/MOSCA/wiki/The-MOSCA-project/_history)

MOSCA was developed to perform metagenomics, metatranscriptomics and metaproteomics data analyses. It was built with Snakemake, and aims to perform all the major steps of meta-omics analyses, together with obtaining as much information as possible. In MOSCA:

## 🧹🗑️ Preprocessing removes undesired reads and regions

Preprocessing combines the power of quality reports by FastQC with low quality reads and regions removal with Trimmomatic and rRNA removal with SortMeRNA, which may be critical for metatrascriptomics analysis. Preprocessing includes:

* Initial quality check with FastQC;
* Illumina artificial sequences removal with Trimmomatic, if adapters were detected by FastQC;
* rRNA removal with SortMeRNA, if metatranscriptomics reads were inputted;
* Quality trimming with Trimmomatic, which includes the removal of reads' tails where quality drops below a critical threshold, and removal of sequences with size or average quality below values inputted by the user;
* Final quality check with FastQC.

The final outputs of preprocessing are the preprocessed reads and the initial and final quality reports.

## 🔗 Assembly reconstructs original genomes and transcripts

Assembly reconstructs the original genomes and transcripts from where inputted reads were produced. It includes:

* Assembly with either MetaSPAdes or Megahit - the choice is made by the user;
* Contig quality control with MetaQUAST - for classic metrics such as N50 - and Bowtie2 - for reporting on the percentage of reads used in the assembly

Tipically, a minimum of 50 % of reads should be included in the assembly. Results can vary wildly between MetaSPAdes and Megahit, and so both should potentially be experimented.

The final outputs of assembly are the contigs and the corresponding quality reports.

## 🗃️ Binning further reconstructs original genomes

Binning groups contigs into distinct clusters based on their taxonomic origin. If it is successful enough, it may produce Metagenome-Assembled Genomes (MAGs). It includes:

* Binning with MaxBin2, based on tetranucleotide composition, relative abundance, and marker genes analysis;
* Bin quality control with CheckM.

The final outputs of binning are the bins and the corresponding quality reports.

## 🔍 Annotation identifies genes, and corresponding taxonomy and function

Annotation begins by identifying genes on the contigs or the preprocessed reads (if not performing assembly), and then annotates those genes with two complementary methods, based on both the whole sequences and the domains present in them. It includes:

* Gene calling with FragGeneScan;
* Sequence-based homology annotation with [UPIMAPI](https://github.com/iquasere/UPIMAPI), against the UniProt database (or subsections of it);
* Domain-based homology annotation with [reCOGnizer](https://github.com/iquasere/reCOGnizer), against multiple databases available at CDD: CDD itself, Protein Clusters, TIGRFAM, NCBIfam, Pfam, SMART, COG and KOG;

The final outputs of annotation are the identified genes, and taxonomic and functional characterization at the gene and sample levels, including EC numbers, COG classifications, taxonomy, among many many other fields of information.

## 📊 Metaproteomics Analysis

Metaproteomics analysis builds a MP database with all proteins possibly present in the datasets to serve as reference for Peptide-to-Spectrum matching (PSM) with inputted MP spectra. Protein inference and quantification is then performed from the results of the PSM. It includes:

* Conversion of spectra in raw formats to Mascot Generic Format (MGF);
* Construction of the first MP database, by joining MG genes, reference proteomes downloaded for the taxonomies identified, protease sequence and cRAP database;
* A first round of PSM with SearchCLI, where spectra are aligned against the first MP database;
* Construction of the second MP database, where only proteins that got matched to at least one PSM are included, followed by a second round of PSM with SearchCLI;
* Protein inference and quantification with Peptide-Shaker.

The final output of metaproteomics analysis is the raw spectracounts, obtained from parsing Peptide-Shaker's reports.

## 🧬 Quantification, normalization and differential expression analysis uncover communities' dynamics

Genomes abundance and gene expression are quantified by aligning MG and MT reads to genomes and genes obtained in MG, respectively.

* Alignment of MG reads to MG contigs with Bowtie2;
* Alignment of MT reads to MG genes with Bowtie2 (if MT reads were inputted);
* Normalization of MG and MT readcounts with edgeR - either TMM or RLE normalization methods can be used;
* Normalization of MP spectracounts with Variance Stabilizing Normalization (if MP spectra were inputted);
* Local Least Squares Imputation of missing MP quantification;
* Differential gene or protein expression analysis with DESeq2 or Reproducibility-Optimized Test Statistics, respectively.

The final outputs of these steps are the normalized quantifications, and the heatmaps and other plots from differential expression analysis.

## 🗺️ Metabolic Maps representation allows to better understand communities' dynamics

[KEGGCharter](https://github.com/iquasere/KEGGCharter) uses the information obtained in previous steps to represent genomic potential and different expression in metabolic maps from KEGG. The final outputs of KEGGCharter are additional functional information from KEGG, including EC numbers and KOs, and the maps, both representing genomic potential and representing differential gene/protein expression.
