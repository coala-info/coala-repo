SafeSim consists of two executables: SafeMix and SafeMut which are both UMI-aware. SafeMix randomly pick reads from two input BAM files and then put the picked reads into the output BAM file. SafeMut randomly pick reads from one input BAM file, spike-in mutations into the picked reads, and then put the mutated reads into the output BAM file. We evaluated SafeMut but not SafeMix because we observed that the performance of SafeMix strongly depends on the initial quantity of DNA. If the initial quantities of DNA in the two input BAM files are quite different from each other, then SafeMix performs poorly with its default parameters, but different samples usually have quite different quantities of DNA in clinical settings. Therefore, we had to figure out a way to balance the initial quantities of DNA at each variant locus first, but this is a hard problem mainly because copy-number alternations and tumor burdens are hard to estimate in clinical settings. SafeMut, on the other hand, is not affected by the problem of DNA-quantity imbalance. Thus, we incorporated UMI-awareness and allele-fraction overdispersion into SafeMut, and we showed that SafeMut performs well with these two techniques.
