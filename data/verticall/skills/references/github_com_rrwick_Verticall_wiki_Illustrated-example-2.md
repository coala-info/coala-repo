# Illustrated example 2

[Jump to bottom](#wiki-pages-box)

Ryan Wick edited this page Mar 21, 2023
·
[14 revisions](/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/Illustrated-example-2/_history)

This page illustrates the [Verticall pairwise](/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/Verticall-pairwise) process using a real pair of assemblies:

* [INF357](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/biosample/SAMEA3357260): a *Klebsiella pneumoniae* isolate
* [KSB1\_8D](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/biosample/SAMEA3357320): a *Klebsiella variicola* isolate where some parts of the genome have been replaced by *Klebsiella pneumoniae* sequence

The second assembly (KSB1\_8D) was also used in [Illustrated example 1](/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/Illustrated-example-1). There it was compared against a *K. variicola* assembly, so its horizontally-acquired *K. pneumoniae* content was more distant than its vertical content. This example is the opposite: it's compared against a *K. pneumoniae* assembly, so its horizontally-acquired *K. pneumoniae* content is closer than its vertical content.

While I went into lots of detail in [Illustrated example 1](/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/Illustrated-example-1), I'll keep things a bit briefer here and in subsequent examples.

## Distribution

Here is the distance distribution with the smoothing and partitioning shown:

![Example 2 distribution](https://github.com/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/images/example_2_distribution.png)

Since this distribution doesn't have a local maximum to the right of the main peak, there are no *t*high and *t*v-high thresholds. This means Verticall isn't considering anything to be horizontal because too much divergence in this pair.

Also note the square-root transform on the x-axis. So while the right peak looks roughly twice as massive as the left peak, it's actually more than seven times as massive, because its bars are more densely packed.

## Painted contigs

![Example 2 painted contigs KSB1_8D](https://github.com/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/images/example_2_painted_contigs_KSB1_8D.png)

## Distance

The [mean distance](/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/Columns-in-pairwise-tsv-file) between these two isolates (using the entirety of their alignments) is 0.04674. This is similar to the value you'd get using [Mash](https://mash.readthedocs.io/en/latest/) (distance=0.04472) or [FastANI](https://github.com/ParBLiSS/FastANI) (identity=95.275%, distance=0.04725).

However, that distance includes the horizontally-acquired regions, so if we want the *vertical* distance (i.e. the distance using only the vertically-inherited parts of the genome), it will be too low. Verticall's [mean vertical distance](/rrwick/Verticall/wiki/Columns-in-pairwise-tsv-file) only uses the vertically-painted parts of the alignments and gives a higher distance of 0.05182.
