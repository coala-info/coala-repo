For the standard pipeline, dammit needs ~18GB of storage space to store its prepared databases, plus a few hundred MB per BUSCO database. For the standard annotation pipeline, we recommend at least 16GB of RAM. This can be reduced by editing LAST parameters via a custom configuration file. The full pipeline, which uses uniref90, needs several hundred GB of space and considerable RAM to prepare the databases. You'll also want either a fat internet connection or a big cup of patience to download uniref. For some species, we have found that the amount of RAM required can be proportional to the size of the transcriptome being annotated.
