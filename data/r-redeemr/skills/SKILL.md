# r-redeemr

## Overview
The `redeemr` R package is the downstream analytical component of the ReDeeM platform. It is designed to process mitochondrial mutation calls (typically generated by the `redeemV` bash/python pipeline) to facilitate fine-scale lineage tracing at the single-cell level. It enables subclonal and phylogenetic analyses while integrating joint RNA and ATAC profiling data to connect genealogies with cell states.

## Installation
Install the development version from GitHub:
```R
devtools::install_github("chenweng1991/redeemR")
library(redeemR)
```

## Core Workflows

### 1. Mutation Filtering (Filter-2 Strategy)
The "Filter-2" strategy is the recommended approach to eliminate position biases and low-molecule artifacts.
- **Edge Trimming**: Remove mutations within a specific distance from fragment ends (default `d = 5`).
- **Binomial Modeling**: Assume residual noise follows a binomial distribution. Use a chi-squared test to filter mutations where the null hypothesis of a binomial distribution cannot be rejected (adjusted p-value < 0.05).
- **Consensus Filtering**: Retain mutations with high consensus scores (typically > 75%) to mitigate formaldehyde-induced damage or gap-filling errors.

### 2. Lineage Tracing and Phylogeny
- **Variant Refinement**: Refine somatic mtDNA mutations to identify high-confidence markers for lineage tracing.
- **Tree Building**: Construct single-cell phylogenetic trees based on refined mitochondrial variants.
- **Clonal Analysis**: Resolve subclonal structures and track cell lineages across different cell states.

### 3. Multi-omic Integration
- **Joint Analysis**: Combine mtDNA-based lineage information with RNA-seq (gene expression) and ATAC-seq (chromatin accessibility) data.
- **Regulatory Circuits**: Map gene regulatory changes onto the reconstructed cell genealogies.

## Key Considerations
- **NUMT Control**: The package implements strategies to minimize the impact of Nuclear Mitochondrial DNA segments (NUMTs). It requires mtDNA mutations to have at least two alleles (eUMIs) in at least one cell.
- **Mutational Signatures**: Real mtDNA mutations are enriched in transitions. Use mutational signature analysis as a validation step for filtered variants.
- **Input Data**: Requires output from the `redeemV` pipeline, which handles the initial mapping and deep mutation calling.
