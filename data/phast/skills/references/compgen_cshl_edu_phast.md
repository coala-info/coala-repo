#### **Phylogenetic Analysis with Space/Time Models**

#### aboutPHAST

Phylogenetic Analysis with Space/Time models (PHAST) is a freely available software package consisting of a collection of command-line programs and supporting libraries for comparative and evolutionary genomics. Best known as the search engine behind the Conservation tracks in the University of California, Santa Cruz (UCSC) Genome Browser, PHAST also includes several tools for phylogenetic modeling, functional element identification, as well as utilities for manipulating alignments, trees and genomic annotations.

The PHAST project was initiated by Principal Investigator, [Adam Siepel](http://siepellab.labsites.cshl.edu/) with extensions by Melissa Hubisz.

Many phast functions can be accessed from within the R environment using the rphast package. It is available from the [RPHAST](http://compgen.cshl.edu/rphast/) webpage and also on [CRAN](https://cran.r-project.org/package%3Drphast).

The [phastWeb](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phastweb/) application was developed by Ritika Ramani.

#### Major PHAST programs

[phastCons](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/phastCons.txt): Conservation scoring and identification of conserved elements

[phyloFit](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/phyloFit.txt): Fitting of phylogenetic models to aligned DNA sequences

[phyloP](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/phyloP.txt): Computation of p-values for conservation or acceleration, either lineage-specific or across all branches

[phastOdds](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/phastOdds.txt): Log-odds scoring for phylogenetic models or phylo-HMMs

[exoniphy](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/exoniphy.txt): Phylogenetic exon prediction

[dless](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/dless.txt): Prediction of elements under lineage-specific selection

[prequel](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/prequel.txt): Probabilistic reconstruction of ancestral sequences

[phastBias](http://compgen.cshl.edu/phast/help-pages/phastBias.txt): Identification of GC-biased gene conversion using a phylo-HMM
